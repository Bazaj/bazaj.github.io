THE ELEPHANTS

The Big Friendly Giants

Matriarchy in Elephants

In elephant groups, the oldest females, often called matriarchs, lead the group. They are responsible for the group's memory, including locations for water and food, and they guide migrations during drought periods.

Social Structure of Elephants

How are they organized?

Elephants live in family groups composed of related females and their young. Males leave the family group upon reaching puberty and live alone or in small all-male groups.

Communication Among Elephants

The basis of cooperation

Elephants communicate using low-frequency sounds called infrasounds, which can travel long distances. They also use visual, tactile, and chemical signals to maintain social bonds and coordinate group movements.

Care and Support Behaviors

Elephants are known for their altruistic behavior and ability to help injured or sick members of the group. Matriarchs play a crucial role in passing down survival knowledge.

Interesting Facts About Elephants

  • Elephants have an exceptional memory, which allows them to remember the location of vital resources for many years.
  • Their large ears help dissipate heat and keep the animal cool in hot climates.
  • Elephants can recognize themselves in a mirror, a trait shared with only a few other animal species.
  • Elephants have complex death rituals, showing behaviors that suggest mourning for deceased group members.
  • Elephants' tusks, which are actually elongated incisor teeth, continue to grow throughout their lifetime.

SIMILARITIES AND DIFFERENCES

  • LIONS
  • Female leadership in social structures.
  • Strong social bonds among females.
  • Females play a major role in the care and upbringing of the young.
  • Lion prides are generally smaller than elephant herds.
  • Lion prides are more territorial.
  • Lions primarily rely on roars and other vocal sounds.
  • ELEPHANTS
  • Female leadership in social structures.
  • Strong social bonds among females.
  • Females play a major role in the care and upbringing of the young.
  • Elephant herds are generally larger than lion prides.
  • Elephant herds are often migratory, moving over large distances in search of food and water.
  • Elephants use a wide range of vocalizations, body language, and infrasound for communication.
