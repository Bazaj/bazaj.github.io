THE LIONS AND LIONESSES

Kings and Queens of the savannah

Lions and Matriarchy

The queens

Lions live in social groups called prides. In a pride, the dominant female, often called the pride's matriarch, plays a crucial role in leading the group. She is responsible for organizing hunts and protecting the pride's territory.

Social Structure

How are they organized?

Within a lion pride, females are typically related and work together to raise the young. Male lions, known as the pride's coalition, defend the territory and mate with the females. This social structure ensures the survival of the pride and its offspring.

Hunting Behavior

Royalty of the wild

Lions are apex predators and hunt in coordinated groups. They often stalk prey together, utilizing teamwork to increase their chances of success. Lionesses do most of the hunting, while male lions protect the pride and sometimes join in on hunts for larger prey.

Interesting Facts About Lions

  • Lions are the only truly social cats, living in prides that can range from 3 to 40 individuals.
  • Male lions are distinguishable by their majestic manes, which vary in color and length.
  • Lions spend much of their time resting, with females doing most of the hunting at night.
  • Despite being apex predators, lions face threats such as habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict.
  • Lions have powerful roars that can be heard up to 5 miles away, allowing them to communicate across long distances.
