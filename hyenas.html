THE HYENAS

The second largest savannah predator

Matriarchy in Hyenas

Spotted hyenas live in matriarchal societies where females dominate males and are often larger and more aggressive. Female leadership is essential for the stability of the clan.

Social Structure of Hyenas

How are they organized?

Spotted hyenas live in clans that can include up to 80 individuals. The social hierarchy is very rigid, with females dominating males and alpha females occupying top positions.

Communication Among Hyenas

The basis of cooperation

Hyenas communicate through a range of distinctive vocalizations, including the famous "laugh" which serves as an alarm and coordination signal during hunts. They also use olfactory and visual signals to maintain social order.

Hunting Behaviors

Hyenas are efficient predators and hunt in coordinated groups. They are known for their ability to hunt large prey such as wildebeest and zebras. Contrary to popular belief, hyenas are more hunters than scavengers.

Interesting Facts About Hyenas

  • Spotted hyenas have one of the strongest bites among mammals, capable of crushing bones.
  • Female hyenas have external genitalia similar to males, an adaptation that still raises many questions among biologists.
  • Hyenas have an extremely varied diet that includes meat, bones, and even vegetable tissues.
  • Spotted hyenas can live up to 25 years in the wild, one of the longest among terrestrial carnivores.
  • Hyena cubs are born with teeth and are immediately ready to compete for maternal milk.

SIMILARITIES AND DIFFERENCES

  • LIONS
  • Female leadership in social structures.
  • Cooperative hunting strategies to capture prey.
  • Lion prides are smaller and have less rigid social structures.
  • Lionesses have a more straightforward reproductive system.
  • Lions have more stable internal relationships with occasional fights for dominance.
  • HYENAS
  • Female leadership in social structures.
  • Cooperative hunting strategies to capture prey.
  • Hyena clans are larger and more complex, with a strict dominance hierarchy among females.
  • Hyenas have a unique genital structure in females and a more complex mating system.
  • Hyenas are more aggressive and have frequent conflicts within the clan.
