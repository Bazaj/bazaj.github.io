THE BEES

Insect kingdom

Matriarchy in Bees

Bees live in matriarchal colonies where a single queen lays all the eggs and the workers, who are all female, maintain and protect the hive. The queen is central to the colony's survival and success.

Social Structure of Bees

How are they organized?

A bee colony consists of one queen, several thousand workers, and a small number of drones (males). Workers perform various tasks, including foraging for food, caring for larvae, and defending the hive.

Communication Among Bees

The basis of cooperation

Bees primarily communicate through dance, such as the waggle dance, which indicates the direction and distance of food sources. They also use pheromones to signal alarms, recognition, and other social functions.

Breeding and Production Behaviors

The queen lays eggs in hexagonal wax cells constructed by the workers. Workers feed the larvae royal jelly, pollen, and nectar, depending on their developmental stage. Bees produce honey as a food source for the winter.

Interesting Facts About Bees

  • Honey bees can visit up to 100 flowers during a single foraging trip.
  • A single honey bee produces only about one-twelfth of a teaspoon of honey in its lifetime.
  • Bees are responsible for pollinating about one-third of the world's food crops.
  • Bees communicate the presence of a new food source through a complex dance that conveys precise information about the location.
  • Worker bees can live up to six weeks during the active season, while queen bees can live up to five years.

SIMILARITIES AND DIFFERENCES

  • LIONS
  • Female leadership in social structures.
  • Cooperative care of the young by related lionesses.
  • Multiple females can reproduce.
  • Lions are mammals with more fluid social roles.
  • Lionesses have varied roles, including hunting and defending the pride.
  • BEES
  • Female leadership in social structures.
  • Cooperative care of the young by all workers.
  • Only the queen reproduces.
  • Bees are insects with highly specialized roles within the colony.
  • The queen bee's lifespan and role are entirely centered around reproduction.
