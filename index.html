What are they?

PATRIARCHAL SOCIETIES

Starting out

Before explaining what matriarchal societies are in nature, it's necessary to understand what the more common patriarchal societies are and how they function. A patriarchal society in nature refers to a social structure in which males hold dominant roles in terms of leadership, authority, and decision-making within a group. Some common feautures are the establishment of social ranks through competition, control over mating for alpha males and shows of strength and combat.

MATRIARCHAL SOCIETIES

What are they?

A matriarchal society in nature refers to a social structure where females hold dominant roles in leadership, authority, and decision-making within a group. These societies are often characterized by strong matrilineal ties and cooperative behaviors among females. Some common features are matrilineal lines, which means that leadership is passed down through female family members without competition, the matriarch taking decisions and general cooperation in management and coordination in defense and attack.

DIFFERENCES

  • PATRIARCHAL
  • Male dominance in authority.
  • Social rank and hierarchy determined by competition.
  • Competition and struggle for dominance.
  • MATRIARCHAL
  • Female dominance in authority.
  • Social hierarchies based on matrilineal lines.
  • Cooperation and bonds between females.
